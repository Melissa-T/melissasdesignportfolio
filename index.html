Her Story

Background


She was born and bred in beautiful Cape Town, South Africa. In December 2017, she left behind her family and friends to pursue her dreams of travelling and living abroad, and moved to the Netherlands with her husband. She decided to make a career switch from doctor to designer and allow her inner creative spirit to flourish.





Design


In December 2018 she graduated from the Codaisseur Academy in Amsterdam with a Certificate in User Experience and User Interface Design. She is passionate about UX research, and excels at storytelling, presentations, and sprint facilitation.

ScaleUpPriorities



Screencast of Design Prototype


The Challenge:


Within 2 weeks I created the design for a self-assessment survey tool called ScaleUpPriorities, to be developed in 2019 by ScaleUpNation (a company that helps ventures that promote sustainabilty be successful at scaling from start-up/stall-up to scale-up).




Click here to see the design brief



The Process:


Inititally it was unclear what I needed to create, so I got further input from the team leader & formulated a clear vision of what the purpose of the tool was and what they wanted the MVP to include. I was the only team member actively working on the project at the time and was unsure if what I was doing would meet their expectations, but I overcame this by collaborating with the product team (product owner and 2 developers) throughout the design process, and together we reached a final design that everyone involved was pleased with.




Examples from design process



My Learnings:


1. Clear communication & cross-functional collaboration is vital for a successful project outcome.
2. Know your capabilities within the given time constraints and don’t take on too much.
3. Define goals to achieve expeted project outcomes.
4. Have the courage to ask for clarity when you are unsure, or ask for help when needed.

OnePlanetConnect



Screencast of Design Prototype


The Challenge:


OnePlanetCrowd BV is a crowdfunding platform where sustainable ventures can seek investors. I created a new product concept within 1 week that would further promote their mission of more sustainable development.




Some Research Findings





Summary of UX Research





Problem identified from UX research





The Process:


I started by internal analysis of the Oneplanetcrowd platform to understand the value of using their website, so that my new product would add to that value for the social entrepreneurs. From my research I decided on developing a mentoring website called Oneplanetconnect. This would help address the problems I identified in my reasearch of why many social ventures fail. I completed the design and conducted usability testing, as well as A/B and multivariantt testing on specific design elements to make iterations and improve on the design.

This project was my final graduation assignment that showcases many of the skills I learnt from the intensive 10 week design course at Codaisseur. Takeaway.com Re-design



Screencast of the Re-design Prototype


The Challenge:


Over a two week period, I developed a re-design for the popular Netherland's online delivery service, Thuisbezorgd.



The Process:


The first week was focused on researching the company, it's competitors, global trends and gathering qualitative & quantitative data from their target market in the form of surveys and interviews. At the end of the week, I developed a problem statement that would address the main issues I identified from my research.




UX Research Process to reach the Problem Statement



The following week I completed a re-design of their website which incorporated a more health-conscious approach and makes it more user-friendly. I did usability testing, as well as A/B and multivariant testing of my re-design, and after some iterations I presented the final product.




One screen of the Re-design

1. I learnt to adapt the skills I had gained from my studies and experience as a doctor, and apply them to UX reserach methods (namely qualitative & quantitative research, interpretation of results, interviewing and presenting skills).
2. I realised I enjoyed UX research and usablity testing of my prototypes (I love interacting with people, and love to apply my analytical mind to results).

Screencast of Prototype


The Challenge:


Create a Tinder-like application within 1 week.

The Process:


Together with 3 developers, we came up with a start-up concept called the Gamerholics. We created the Co-Op application where gamers can network with other gamers in their community or globally, depending on their preferences.



Creating IA that is transparent & user-centred





Wireframes with IA incorporating the features & user flow decided on by the Gamerholics team





A closer look at a wireframe with IA



By incorporating Sprint 2.0 techniques into our workflow, and maintaining good communication throughout the process with the help of tools like Slack, Trello and morning stand-ups, we were able to work well as a team and achieve the MVP while having a lot of fun along the way.



The Trello Co-Op board of our team. We used this for resource sharing, planning and communication




My Learnings:


1. I learnt to convey my concepts to developers & adapt a sprint to suite our project & team needs.

3. I spent time to create a detailed information architecture for Co-Op so the entire team would understand it.
