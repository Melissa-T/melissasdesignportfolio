ScaleUpPriorities




The Challenge:


Within 2 weeks I created the design for a self-assessment survey tool called ScaleUpPriorities, to be developed in 2019 by ScaleUpNation (a company that helps ventures that promote sustainabilty be successful at scaling from start-up/stall-up to scale-up).


The Process:


Inititally it was unclear what I needed to create, so I got further input from the team leader & formulated a clear vision of what the purpose of the tool was and what they wanted the MVP to include. I was the only team member actively working on the project at the time and was unsure if what I was doing would meet their expectations, but I overcame this by collaborating with the product team (product owner and 2 developers) throughout the design process, and together we reached a final design that everyone involved was pleased with.


My Learnings:



1. Clear communication & cross-functional collaboration is vital for a successful project outcome.

2. Know your capabilities within the given time constraints and don’t take on too much.

3. Define goals to achieve expeted project outcomes.

4. Have the courage to ask for clarity when you are unsure, or ask for help when needed.

OnePlanetConnect


The Challenge:


OnePlanetCrowd BV is a crowdfunding platform where sustainable ventures can seek investors. I created a new product concept within 1 week that would further promote their mission of more sustainable development.



The Process:


I started by internal analysis of the Oneplanetcrowd platform to understand the value of using their website, so that my new product would add to that value for the social entrepreneurs. From my research I decided on developing a mentoring website called Oneplanetconnect. This would help address the problems I identified in my reasearch of why many social ventures fail. I completed the design and conducted usability testing, as well as A/B and multivariantt testing on specific design elements to make iterations and improve on the design.

This project was my final graduation assignment that showcases many of the skills I learnt from the intensive 10 week design course at Codaisseur. Tony


The Challenge:



Create the design for a Slack integration application in 1 week.



The Process:


Together with 2 designers, we developed a start-up concept called Tony & Co. and created Tony, a message tone analyzer application for Slack. My main roles during the project included logo and icon design, as well as scripting the presentation of our application to colleagues (designers and developers). 2. Working with other designers & learning to comprimise when you disagree to keep moving forward.

3. Morning standups are important. They are a great way for the team to maintain communication throughout the project.

Takeaway.com Re-design


The Challenge:


Within 2 weeks I created the design for a self-assessment survey tool called ScaleUpPriorities, to be developed in 2019 by ScaleUpNation (a company that helps ventures that promote sustainabilty be successful at scaling from start-up/stall-up to scale-up).


The Process:


The first week was focused on researching the company, it's competitors, global trends and gathering qualitative & quantitative data from their target market in the form of surveys and interviews. At the end of the week, I developed a problem statement that would address the main issues I identified from my research. The following week I completed a re-design of their website which incorporated a more health-conscious approach and makes it more user-friendly. I did usability testing, as well as A/B and multivariant testing of my re-design, and after some iterations I presented the final product.

Co-Op


The Challenge:


Create a Tinder-like application within 1 week.


The Process:


Together with 3 developers, we came up with a start-up concept called the Gamerholics. We created the Co-Op application where gamers can network with other gamers in their community or globally, depending on their preferences. By incorporating Sprint 2.0 techniques into our workflow, and maintaining good communication throughout the process with the help of tools like Slack, Trello and morning stand-ups, we were able to work well as a team and achieve the MVP while having a lot of fun along the way.


My Learnings:



1. I learnt to convey my concepts to developers & adapt a sprint to suite our project & team needs.

2. I learnt I enjoyed collaborating within a team environment, and I like to take on a leadership role.

3. I spent time to create a detailed information architecture for Co-Op so the entire team would understand it.
